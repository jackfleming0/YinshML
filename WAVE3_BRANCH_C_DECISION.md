# Wave 3 Branch C — Decision Doc

One bet, one falsification experiment. Branches A and B/B' answered "how do we stop damaging the seed?" — the pipeline is now stable. Branch C asks **"how do we make training meaningfully exceed seed quality?"** — a structural question, not a knob-tuning question.

---

## The problem, in one sentence

After Wave 3 Branches A, B, and B', the pipeline is **stable but non-constructive**: 5/5 iters promoted, final model matches seed at 67.5% MCTS-48, but no iter beat seed by a margin outside CI noise.

---

## What the data tells us about WHY

Three measurements drove this:

**1. The policy head doesn't internalize MCTS gains.**

| Eval mode (iter 0 EMA, n=60) | WR vs heuristic d1 |
|---|---:|
| Raw policy (no search) | 16.7% |
| MCTS-48 (training-config search) | 63.3% |
| MCTS-400 (deployment-config search) | 81.7% |

65-point gap between raw policy and MCTS-400. The value head + search at depth is doing nearly all the work; the policy head alone barely beats chance. 5 iters of training did not narrow this gap.

**2. The training step doesn't move the value head past seed.**

Both Branch B and Branch B' end iter 4 at MCTS-48 WR within CI of seed (67.5%). Across 1000 games of self-play training + 5 epochs of value-head SGD, the value head ends up about as accurate as where it started.

**3. Self-play data quality is the natural suspect.**

Self-play is generated by MCTS-48 from the current network. MCTS-48 takes the seed's policy prior + value head and produces visit distributions / terminal outcomes that get distilled back. **The teacher (MCTS-48) is only marginally better than the student (raw policy)** at improving over the seed. Whatever signal MCTS-48 has, it's not enough to push the network above the seed plateau.

Compare: MCTS-400 wins 81.7% vs the heuristic, MCTS-48 wins 63.3%. **An 18-point gap in teacher quality** between the search depth we *use* in self-play and the search depth available at deployment. If the network were trained against MCTS-400 targets, the policy distillation target distribution would be meaningfully stronger.

## Candidate architectural bets

Surveying the WAVE3_BRANCHES.md menu plus what the data has surfaced:

| # | Bet | What it tests | Cost | Risk |
|---|---|---|---:|---|
| C1 | **Increase self-play search depth (48 → 200 sims)** | Is self-play target quality the bottleneck? Stronger MCTS teacher → stronger candidate. | ~$25-40, ~50-70h cloud. | High. Slow; if it doesn't help, we burn the run budget. |
| C2 | **Two-stage curriculum: train head against MCTS-48, then refine against MCTS-400** | Same as C1 but split: cheap exploration → expensive refinement. | ~$30-50. | Similar; more complex. |
| C3 | **Decouple policy head from value head** (train value from outcomes; train policy from MCTS visit dist with separate LR) | Is one head limiting the other? | Day of dev work + $10-15 run. | Medium. Standard pattern; well-grounded. |
| C4 | **Supervised continued pretraining from expert game data (BGA/Boardspace)** | Is the bottleneck training-data distribution rather than recipe? | 2-3 days dev (per memory the pipeline is built but unrun). | Medium. Different beast — supervised, not self-play. |
| C5 | **Pure AlphaZero from scratch (no seed)** | Is the seed itself a local optimum self-play can't escape? | 1-2 weeks compute. | High. Long horizon, easy to abandon. |
| C6 | **Larger network capacity** | Is the network architecturally bottlenecked? | $20-30 + retraining the seed. | High. Changes everything at once. |

## The bet — C1 with a tighter experiment

**The bet**: training-data quality is the bottleneck. With MCTS-200 self-play (instead of MCTS-48) as the teacher, the network will train past the seed at MCTS-48 anchor eval.

**Why this and not C3/C4**:
- C1 is the most direct test of what §8 actually measured. We have hard data showing MCTS-400 is much stronger than MCTS-48; using something in between (MCTS-200) is a natural intervention.
- C3 (head decoupling) is theoretically clean but doesn't have direct empirical support — it's a "tidy the recipe" change, not a hypothesis-driven one.
- C4 (expert data) is high-value but a different experiment (supervised, not self-play). Better as a parallel track than as Branch C.
- C5 (from scratch) takes too long to falsify cheaply.
- C6 (larger network) changes too many variables at once.

**Why not full MCTS-400 in self-play (matching §8's deployment depth)**:
- 400 sims/move × 80 moves/game × 200 games = 6.4M sims per iter. At ~250 sims/sec on a 4090, that's ~7h of pure search per iter, so ~35h+ for 5 iters of self-play alone. Plus training. Total run ~50-70h, ~$25-35. Too expensive to test the hypothesis cleanly.
- MCTS-200 is a midpoint: 4× the seed search cost (so ~12-15h self-play across 5 iters) but 4× stronger targets than MCTS-48. Better signal-to-cost ratio.

## The falsification experiment

**Recipe**: `configs/wave3_branchC_mcts200.yaml` (to be written). Same as Branch B' (epochs=1, warmup=1, threshold=0.20) with:

```diff
 self_play:
-  num_simulations: 48
-  late_simulations: 32
+  num_simulations: 200
+  late_simulations: 100
```

Everything else identical to B'.

**Expected wall time**: 5 iters × ~3h self-play + ~5 min train + ~25 min anchor = ~17-19h per iter at MCTS-200. Wait — MCTS-200 is ~4× the search cost of MCTS-48. B' self-play was ~3h/iter. So C1 should be ~12h/iter × 5 = 60h. Too slow.

**Reduce to 100 games/iter** to fit in a 24-30h budget. Smaller buffer means iter-to-iter signal is noisier, but anchor n=40 is still a fixed measurement. Expected wall time: 5 iters × ~6h = ~30h, ~$15-20.

**Success criteria** (any one suffices):
- Best iter anchor MCTS-48 ≥ 75% (n=40) — exceeds seed's 67.5% with CI lower bound above seed mean.
- Best iter raw policy ≥ 30% (n=40) — meaningful policy-head improvement (vs current 16-20%).
- Mean iter anchor MCTS-48 ≥ 65% (n=40) AND highest single iter ≥ 70% — both higher floor and higher ceiling than B'.

**Failure criteria**:
- Best iter anchor MCTS-48 ≤ 67.5% (n=40) AND mean ≤ 57% — MCTS-200 didn't change the plateau. Target quality wasn't the bottleneck; something deeper (decoupled heads, network capacity, distribution shift) is.

**If C1 succeeds**: a full MCTS-400 self-play run becomes worth running. Or a longer (10-15 iter) run at MCTS-200 to compound the gains.

**If C1 fails**: the bottleneck isn't target search depth. Next bet is C3 (head decoupling) — cheap to implement and has a different theoretical underpinning.
